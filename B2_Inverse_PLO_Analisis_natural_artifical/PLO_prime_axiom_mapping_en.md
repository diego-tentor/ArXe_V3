## Bottom-up analysis of the PLO corpus

## The method

Every constant in the PLO corpus has a prime factorization. If a prime appears
in multiple constants from different sectors, that co-presence is not accidental:
it signals that all those constants share a **common axiomatic choice**.

We build the inverted index: prime → constants that contain it.
Then we ask: what physical decision unites this group?

## The emerging hierarchy

### Layer 1 — Fundamental ontological axioms (primes 2, 3, 5, 7)

Appear in almost all constants. They are the conditions of possibility of all physics.

| Prime | Axiom | Content |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 2 | **DIFFERENTIATION** | That distinction exists — that there are at least two possible states |
| 3 | **CYCLICITY** | That irreversible process exists / that time has a direction |
| 5 | **MEMORY** | That persistent state exists / that information storage is possible |
| 7 | **SPATIALITY** | That space has exactly 3 dimensions |

Prime 7 is the most informative in this layer. It appears in Ω_m, V_ud, V_us, α, α_s, m_W, m_d, m_e — constants from completely different sectors. What unites them: all describe phenomena that *exist in three-dimensional space*. The axiom is the choice that space has 3 dimensions, not 2 or 4.

### Layer 2 — Field structure axioms (primes 11, 13, 17, 19)

Appear in subsets of the corpus. They are the choices that determine which forces exist.

| Prime | Axiom | Constants that share it |
|-------|-------|------------------------|
| 11 | **EM FIELD**: U(1) exists with universal coupling | α, α_s_ess, G_F, m_b, m_t, sin²θ_W, θ₁₂, n_s |
| 13 | **SU(2) ISOSPIN**: the weak force exists | α, V_ud, S8_cmb |
| 17 | **MASS / YUKAWA**: Higgs coupling exists | α, m_e, m_t, G_N, V_us |
| 19 | **GENERATIONS**: multiple fermion families exist | m_μ, m_s, m_b, m_W, V_us, θ₁₂, Ω_b, n_s |

**Note on prime 19:** it groups exactly the constants that involve
the second generation or that depend on there being more than one generation. m_μ is the second
lepton. m_s is the second down-type quark. θ₁₂ is the first–second generation mixing angle.
The axiom is the choice that nature *repeats* the fermion families.

**Note on prime 17:** it connects m_e, m_t and G_N — the electron, the heaviest
quark, and gravity. What they share: all involve mass generated by
coupling to the Higgs field (in the case of G_N, the connection is to the
Planck scale / vacuum structure). The axiom is the choice that a mass
generation mechanism exists.

### Layer 3 — Symmetry breaking and EW structure axioms (primes 41, 71, 107, 109)

Appear in small groups. They are more specific choices.

| Prime | Axiom | Constants |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| 41 | **VEV**: the EW vacuum has value v = 246 GeV | m_H, m_W, θ₂₃ |
| 71 | **TAU_ID**: third leptonic generation exists | m_τ, G_F |
| 107 | **TOP_ID**: third quark generation exists | m_t, V_ud |
| 109 | **M_Z SCHEME**: μ = M_Z as EW reference scale | m_Z, G_F |

**Prime 41** connects the Higgs and the W — the two objects directly determined
by the VEV of the Higgs field. And also θ₂₃, the largest mixing in the CKM
matrix, which depends on the mass hierarchy that the Higgs generates. The axiom is
the choice of the specific value of the VEV.

**Prime 109** is particularly clean: m_Z and G_F are exactly the two
constants used as inputs to the electroweak renormalization scheme
centered on the Z pole. The axiom is the choice of μ = M_Z as the reference
scale — a purely conventional choice but universally adopted.

## The complete structure

What emerges is not an arbitrary list but a **hierarchy of decisions**,
where each layer presupposes the previous ones:

```
LAYER 1 — Conditions of possibility
  Prime 2: that there is distinction
  Prime 3: that there is irreversible process
  Prime 5: that there is persistent state
  Prime 7: that space is 3D

       ↓ given these axioms, one can choose:

LAYER 2 — Force structure
  Prime 11: that U(1) / electromagnetism exists
  Prime 13: that SU(2) / the weak force exists
  Prime 17: that a mass mechanism (Yukawa) exists
  Prime 19: that multiple generations exist

       ↓ given these axioms, one can choose:

LAYER 3 — Specific symmetry-breaking values
  Prime 41: that the VEV is 246 GeV
  Prime 71: that the tau exists (3rd leptonic generation)
  Prime 107: that the top exists (3rd quark generation)
  Prime 109: that μ = M_Z is the reference scale

       ↓ given these axioms, one can choose:

LAYER 4 — Precision conventions
  Prime 131: that N²LO corrections are included
  Prime 137: that α(0) is the EM reference constant
  Prime 1051: that the Weinberg angle incorporates 3 generations
```

Each prime is the size of the logical space opened by the corresponding decision.
A larger prime does not mean a more important choice —
it means a choice that presupposes more previously fixed layers.

## What this implies for the NI

The Naturality Index (NI = maximum prime in the factorization) now has a
precise axiomatic reading:

> **NI = the deepest axiomatic choice (most layers presupposed) that was
> necessary for this constant to have the value it has.**

A constant with NI=7 (like Ω_m) requires only the choice of 3D spatiality.
A constant with NI=109 (like m_Z) requires: differentiation + cyclicity +
memory + spatiality + U(1) + SU(2) + mass + generations + VEV + M_Z scheme.
That is ten stacked axiomatic choices.

The NI does not count the number of choices — it captures the *depth* of the last one.

## Verification: α as a test case

α has the primes: 3, 7, 11, 13, 17, 137, 421, 521.

Reading axiomatically:
- 3: irreversible process exists (the photon travels)
- 7: space is 3D (the photon propagates in 3D)
- 11: U(1) field exists (the photon is the U(1) boson)
- 13: SU(2) exists (α contains EW corrections involving SU(2))
- 17: mass mechanism exists (Higgs radiative corrections)
- 137: α(0) is the EM reference (self-reference: α defines itself)
- 421, 521: higher-order QED and EW precision corrections

The factorization of α is the complete history of the axiomatic choices
necessary for the electromagnetic coupling to have the value it has.
Each prime is a layer of that history.

## Open prediction

If the axiomatic hierarchy is correct, it should be possible to:

1. Given an unknown constant, predict which primes it should contain
   based on which physical sector it belongs to and which axioms it presupposes.

2. Given a PLO formula with an unexpected prime, identify which
   axiomatic choice was not being accounted for.

3. Identify "misplaced" constants — those containing primes from a layer
   they should not need, signaling that their current value incorporates
   axiomatic decisions that the phenomenon itself does not require.
