## 3. UNIFIED THEORY: THE 5 PRINCIPLES OF AXIOMATIC ARCHAEOLOGY

### Principle 1: REALITY IS NEGOTIATION

There is no direct access to "laws of nature." What we call physical constants are stabilization points in communal negotiations about how to describe observed phenomena.

```

      Institutional_negotiation, Metrological_negotiation)
```

### Principle 2: GRAMMAR RECORDS THE NEGOTIATION

The mathematical structure of a constant encodes its negotiation history:

- **Prime/semi-prime base** → foundational choices
- **Denominators 2^a×5^b×10^c** → choice of decimal system
- **Specific prime factors** → specific theoretical decisions
- **Decimal corrections** → historical refinements
- **Dualities/tensions** → unresolved conflicts

### Principle 3: OBJECTIVITY EMERGES FROM TRIANGULATION

The "objectivity" of a constant is not an intrinsic property but an emergent effect of:

- Multiple independent measurement methods
- Multiple theories predicting the same value
- Institutional consensus (CODATA, PDG)
- Successful predictive capacity

**Objectivity = stability under triangulation**

### Principle 4: TENSIONS ARE FEATURES, NOT BUGS

Persistent experimental tensions (H₀, S₈, etc.) are not anomalies to be eliminated — they are:

- Signals of the need for new negotiation
- Structural features of knowledge
- Opportunities for grammatical expansion
- Reminders of the negotiated nature of every constant

**ΔC has analyzable structure that reveals the nature of the disagreement**

### Principle 5: PHYSICS EVOLVES THROUGH GRAMMATICAL EXPANSION

Scientific progress occurs not through "discovery of truths" but through expansion of the negotiational grammar:

- New evidence → new negotiation required
- New theory → new operators become available

- New consensus → new stabilization reached

**The history of physics is the history of grammatical expansions**
