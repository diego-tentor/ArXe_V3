## PREAMBLE: Why This Articulation Is Necessary

### 1. Classical Physics Assumes Physical Pre-existences

Classical and quantum physics *presupposes* time and space as a given background. It does not articulate them from more basic structures: it installs them as a prior stage upon which phenomena then occur. Newton's spacetime is an absolute container. Einstein's is dynamic, but equally pre-existent as structure. Quantum mechanics' spacetime is the background upon which states and operators are defined.

This choice carries a precise philosophical cost: **it is Platonist**. Assuming that space and time are the given substrate of reality amounts to postulating entities that need not be generated, that simply *are* before any process. They are pre-existences in the sense that no physical theory asks where they come from — they are assumed as a starting point.

ArXe takes a different path: **it articulates them from more basic logical layers**. Not as a consequence of other physical entities, but from the most minimal logical structure possible.

### 2. The Key: Separating Essence from Existence Incorporates Indecidability

The move that makes this articulation possible is a distinction that standard physics does not operate:

> **Essence ≠ Existence ≠ Truth**

Classical physics implicitly operates the co-implication: what *is* also *exists* and is susceptible to *truth*. Under that co-implication, indecidability can only be epistemic — we do not know which of two states is real, but one of them is. The order exists even if we do not know it.

ArXe breaks that co-implication. When essence and existence are separated, indecidability can be **ontological**: there is no order because order does not exist at that level, not because we are ignorant of it. And this completely transforms the landscape. Without decidability there is no first or second. Without first or second, succession is not possible. And if there can be no succession, what there is is **simultaneity** — which is exactly the structure of space.

The separation of essence/existence is not a trick: it is the condition of possibility for the entire structural articulation.

A necessary warning for what follows: the T^k levels articulated below are not temporal steps or stages of an argument. They are **coexisting logical layers** — degrees of determination that are all simultaneously present in the structure of reality. What is articulated is what type of phenomenon each layer can support, not in what order it appeared.

### 3. The Foundation Is Local and Present, Not Transcendent

ArXe is not Platonist because it finds no ultimate foundation in any eternal or ideal domain. The foundation — if it can be called that — is the **present and local temporal act**: the *istence*, the "this, here, now". The contradictory generation does not occur in a "beyond" of pure forms. It occurs exactly here and now, in the only place where anything can occur.

This has a direct consequence: time does not emerge *from* something eternal. It emerges as the first act of stabilization, which is itself temporal. There is no vicious circularity here — there is **structural closure**: the act that generates temporality is itself temporal, not prior to it in an atemporal domain.

### 4. The LEM Is Not Denied — It Is Localized

The most natural objection to ArXe is: "you are violating the Law of Excluded Middle (LEM)". The answer is more precise than a negation: ArXe does not deny the LEM. It **circumscribes** it.

The LEM is correct and operative in the layer of binary logic — level T¹ of the hierarchy. What ArXe rejects is its **universalization**: the pretension that the LEM governs all levels of reality. Indecidability and circularity are not logical errors to be eliminated; they are structural features of levels where binary logic is not yet the operative layer. Classical physics, by assuming the LEM as universal, cannot see those levels. ArXe makes them visible precisely because it limits the LEM's scope to where it belongs.

This is not adjusting the game for convenience. The discussion over whether separating essence from existence is legitimate has centuries of philosophical history — from Aristotle to Avicenna, from Kant to Heidegger, from Frege to contemporary analytic ontology. Classical science made an axiomatic decision by fusing them. That decision made certain phenomena and certain types of order visible. ArXe makes the other axiomatic decision — and makes visible structures the first cannot see. What each framework can observe, describe, and predict depends on the axioms with which it was built. There is no neutral vantage point from which to compare the results of both frameworks without already being inside one of them.

This inverts the usual relation: the increase of entropy is a **consequence** of the ontological irreversibility of T⁻¹, not its cause.

The transition from T⁻¹ to T² is not arbitrary. It is governed by the principle:

```
Logical indecidability at level n
         ⟺
Spatial simultaneity at level n+k
```

**Structural demonstration:**

1. In T¹ there exist two temporal phases `(a, a')` — logically identical

3. This is **indecidability**: no internal factor at the level can break the symmetry
4. If succession cannot be established → they cannot be temporally successive
5. If they cannot be successive → **they must be simultaneous**
6. **Simultaneity = spatial coexistence = spatial structure**

∴ The indecidability of order between identical temporal phases **forces** the emergence of space.

**Why this is possible in ArXe but not in classical physics:**

Classical physics does not operate the essence/existence distinction. For it, indecidability is an epistemic limitation (we do not know the order), not an ontological condition (there *is* no order). ArXe says: indecidability is not ignorance about an order that exists — **order does not exist at that level**. And when order does not exist, the phases *are* simultaneous in an ontological sense, not merely an epistemic one. This ontological simultaneity *is* space.

## ON THE FALSIFIABILITY OF THIS ARTICULATION

This articulation is falsifiable — in Popper's original sense, not in the sense that later tradition assigned to him.

Popper introduced falsifiability as an **epistemic attitude**: the disposition not to defend one's ideas dogmatically, to recognize that they may be wrong, to remain open to rational critique. Not as an experimental protocol demanding a "refuting proof" for a theory to be legitimate. That second reading — that a theory is only valid if it specifies in advance the conditions that would destroy it — is itself a philosophical position, not a neutral fact about science, and it presupposes exactly the binary true/false logic that ArXe circumscribes to a specific layer of reality.

ArXe adopts the original attitude: **this articulation may be wrong**. The axiomatic choices it makes — separating essence from existence, treating indecidability as ontological, circumscribing the LEM to the binary layer — are choices, not certainties. The central argument (indecidability → simultaneity → space) is structurally coherent and philosophically motivated, but coherence is not truth.

What ArXe does not accept is the demand for a "refuting proof" formulated from within the framework being questioned. Asking ArXe to prove its validity using the criteria of classical physics amounts to asking the second axiomatic path to justify itself from the first — which is not neutrality, it is circularity in favor of the dominant framework.

The openness to being wrong is genuine. Dialogue is the mechanism, not experimental falsation as the ultimate arbiter.
