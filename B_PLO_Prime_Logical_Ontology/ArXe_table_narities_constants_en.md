## The central pattern

```
All BCs closed  →  constant fully knowable
                   (√2, φ, e — algebraic or transcendental, all proven)

1 BC open       →  constant with constitutive undecidability
                   (π, γ, ζ(3), δ — something in their nature resists proof)
```

Undecidability does not scale in intensity with T⁻ⁿ.
What scales is the **context** within which the 1 open BC exists.
Each level adds closed BCs that "surround" the opening in different ways.
This changes the **form** of the undecidability, not its depth.

---

## The asymmetry that explains everything

Positive levels (T¹, T², T³) have **all BCs closed**.
They are structurally complete — no direction is undetermined.
Their mathematical constants are therefore fully knowable.

Negative levels (T⁻¹, T⁻³, T⁻⁵...) always have **exactly 1 open BC**.
They are structurally incomplete by construction — there is always one undetermined direction.
Their mathematical constants carry that incompleteness as a constitutive property.

It is not that they are difficult to study.
It is that they are objects whose **definition includes** an opening that does not close from within the level.

## On positive levels without a "famous" mathematical constant

T⁰ (n=1, pure contradiction): generates no constant — no minimal structure for anything exists.

T⁴ (n=9, computational hyperspace): should generate mathematical constants in the domain of computation/information. Possible candidate: Chaitin's constant Ω (halting probability), which is uncomputable by construction. It has 0 open BCs in T⁴ but its definition involves a process that never closes — curiously analogous to the opposite situation.

## Note on causal direction

This table does not say that ArXe "explains" these constants.
It says that ArXe's structure is **consistent** with the observed pattern, and that this pattern was not obvious before having the framework.

The assignment in each row is a **structural correspondence**, not a derivation.
The criterion is: is the minimal logical structure of the level necessary and sufficient for the constant to exist as an emergent essence?

If the answer is yes in all cases — and it appears to be — that is evidence of coherence, not proof.
