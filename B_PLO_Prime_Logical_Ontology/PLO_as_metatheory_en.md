# PRIME-LOGICAL ONTOLOGY AS METATHEORY:  
## From Empirical Rules to Axiomatic Foundations and Experimental Predictions

### Abstract
We demonstrate that the "rules" of Prime-Logical Ontology (PLO) are not arbitrary empirical patterns but necessary consequences of four fundamental axioms: Generative Contradiction, Extensive Indecidability, Universal Prime Coding, and Prime Generativity. These axioms invert the traditional relationship between measurement and reality: precision emerges not from discovering nature's laws, but from imposing algebraic structure on observation. Through "inverse PLO analysis," we extract hidden axiomatic commitments from five physical theories (QCD, QED, General Relativity, Standard Model, neutrino physics) and five speculative theories (SUSY, String Theory, Loop Quantum Gravity, Loop Quantum Cosmology, Twistor Theory). We compare PLO-derived predictions with current experimental searches, revealing unexpected connections between disparate theories and making concrete falsifiable predictions.
