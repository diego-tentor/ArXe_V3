# ArXe Theory: The Logical-Physical Co-emergence of the Universe

## Introduction

Contemporary theoretical physics operates within a foundational asymmetry that is rarely made explicit: our most successful frameworks — quantum mechanics, general relativity, the Standard Model — describe *how* the universe behaves with extraordinary precision, yet none of them address *why* the universe exists, or why it obeys the particular logical and mathematical structure it does. This is not merely a philosophical gap. It represents a structural incompleteness at the base of the entire scientific enterprise.

ArXe Theory proposes a resolution to this incompleteness through a single, radical reorientation: rather than beginning with an existing universe and deriving its laws, it begins with the minimal logical conditions under which existence itself becomes necessary.

---

## The Fundamental Axiom

At the core of ArXe stands a single axiomatic act — hereafter referred to as the **Fundamental Contradictory Act (FCA)** — defined as the simultaneous co-emergence of being and non-being. This is not a creation event situated in time; it is the pre-temporal logical structure that makes time, space, and physical law possible as derived consequences.

The FCA is not postulated arbitrarily. It is motivated by the observation that any attempt to ground reality in a purely positive ontology (something simply *is*) or a purely negative one (nothing simply *is not*) results in infinite regress or logical circularity. The contradictory act — the irreducible tension between existence and non-existence — is the only starting point that is both self-generating and logically stable.

From this single act, the theory develops a **Recursive Fractal Ontology**: a hierarchical structure in which each level of reality emerges as the necessary logical consequence of the one preceding it, corresponding to successive physical dimensions and degrees of structural complexity.

## Structure and Scope

The ontological hierarchy ArXe identifies is the following:

- **T⁰** — Absolute non-existence: the null state from which the FCA arises
- **T¹ / T⁻¹** — Homogeneous positive time / Temporal alterity: the first distinction, corresponding to temporal dimensionality
- **T² / T⁻²** — Spatial anteriority / Spatial variation: the emergence of spatial structure as a second-order consequence of temporal differentiation
- **T³ / T⁻³** — Mass as space-time / Color confinement: the triadic level at which matter and gauge structure become possible

Each level is formally characterised by a **boundary condition algebra** and encoded by the prime number structure associated with its logical depth. The correspondence between this abstract ontological hierarchy and the physical quantities measured in experiment is not analogical — it is proposed as structural identity.

## Relationship to Established Physics

ArXe does not compete with quantum mechanics, general relativity, or the Standard Model. It stands in relation to them as statistical mechanics stands in relation to thermodynamics: the latter correctly describes observable regularities; the former reveals why those regularities could not have been otherwise.

Specifically:

- The gauge symmetries of the Standard Model are reinterpreted as structural consequences of boundary conditions at each ontological level, rather than empirically postulated symmetries.
- The physical constants — including the fine structure constant, the speed of light, and the masses of fundamental particles — are derived, in template form, from the recursive structure of the ontological hierarchy. Their specific numerical values encode the axiomatic history of the measurement frameworks in which they were determined.
- The measurement problem in quantum mechanics finds a structural analogue in the correspondence between logical undecidability and physical simultaneity established in Section 8.

## Epistemic Status and Falsifiability

ArXe operates under an explicit epistemic framework that rejects the classical binary of proof versus refutation, while maintaining rigour. Following Gödelian constraints on formal systems, no theory that is sufficiently expressive to describe physical reality can be both complete and consistent within a purely axiomatic-deductive model. ArXe adopts instead a **dialogical-interpretive standard**: the theory is evaluated by the coherence of its structural mappings, the fruitfulness of its predictions, and its capacity to illuminate questions that standard physics does not ask.

This does not entail unfalsifiability. Section 13 presents concrete, testable predictions — including signatures accessible at the LHC and FCC, precision deviations in measured constants, and novel cosmological phenomena — that would, if falsified, require revision of core structural claims.

## How to Evaluate This Theory

ArXe is not a paper. It is a **theoretical corpus** — a structured set of documents whose arguments are distributed across multiple registers: formal, epistemological, empirical, and philosophical. This has a direct consequence for how the theory should be evaluated: assessing it from a single document, or from any proper subset of the corpus, will systematically produce an incomplete or distorted verdict.

This is not a rhetorical disclaimer. It follows from the nature of what ArXe proposes.

Any theory that attempts to address questions simultaneously at the level of ontological structure, physical prediction, and epistemic framework will necessarily exceed what a single document can carry. Evaluating ArXe's treatment of physical constants without reading the epistemological framework is like evaluating a proof while ignoring its axioms. Evaluating the Prime Logic Grammar without the core ontological hierarchy is like evaluating a notation system without the mathematics it encodes. Each document in the corpus performs a distinct function; none of them is the theory in its entirety.

There is also a more specific risk. Because ArXe operates at a level of abstraction that sits above standard physical formalism, a reader encountering it through only one document is likely to mistake the register of that document for the register of the whole. The core theory document, read alone, may appear more speculative than it is — because the formal constraints that ground it are developed elsewhere. The empirical patterns document, read alone, may appear merely numerological — because the structural logic that motivates those patterns is developed elsewhere. The epistemological framework, read alone, may appear to be hedging — because the concrete predictions it qualifies are developed elsewhere.

The minimum corpus for a fair evaluation is the following:

- [The Logical-Physical Co-emergence of the Universe](https://github.com/diego-tentor/ArXe_V3/blob/master/1_Core/arxe_1_core_theory_V3_en.md)
- [Summary](https://github.com/diego-tentor/ArXe_V3/blob/master/summary.md)

A reader who has engaged with all of these is in a position to assess ArXe on its own terms — which is the only evaluation that is methodologically defensible. A reader who has engaged with fewer is in a position to form provisional impressions, which may be useful, but should be held lightly.

We ask only for the standard that any serious theoretical proposal deserves: to be read as a whole before being judged as a whole.
