We examine three case studies (top quark mass, Hubble constant, QCD scale) and discuss implications for scientific methodology and philosophy of physics.

### 3.5 Limitations and Alternative Explanations

**We acknowledge several limitations:**

1. **Sample size:** Current analysis covers 11 constants in detail, 47 in statistical analysis. Larger sample needed to rule out selection bias.

2. **Post-hoc pattern recognition:** Most patterns identified after examining data, not predicted a priori. Risk of overfitting.

**Our response:** While this is logically possible, it requires explaining:
- Why mathematical unity manifests preferentially in base-10 arithmetic (Pattern 2)
- Why "unity" appears only after scientific consensus, not before (predictive failures, Section 6.2)
- Why grammatical structures reorganize during paradigm shifts rather than remaining stable (Pattern 1)

The **convergence of multiple independent patterns** (Bayesian accumulation of evidence) makes construction hypothesis more parsimonious than Platonic mathematical realism.

## 4. Theoretical Implications

### 4.1 Constants as Negotiated Agreements

Our findings suggest a reconceptualization of physical constants:

**Traditional view:**
> "Constants are discovered values representing intrinsic properties of nature. Science converges toward these values as measurement and theory improve."

**Archaeological view:**
> "Constants are stabilized agreements between theoretical frameworks about how to numerically express successful predictions. Science stabilizes these values as communities reach consensus on interpretative axioms."

**Key difference:** Not "discovered vs. invented" but **"found in nature vs. found in discourse about nature."**

### 4.2 The Precision Paradox

A central finding: **Precision is necessarily axiomatic.**

**Why constants cannot be calculated "in abstract":**

1. **Underdetermination:** Any finite set of measurements is compatible with infinitely many theoretical interpretations
2. **Framework dependence:** "The mass of the top quark" means different things in:
   - Pole mass scheme
   - MS-bar scheme  
   - On-shell scheme
   
   These give *different numerical values* for "the same" quark
   
3. **Precision requires choice:** To calculate m_t to 0.01 GeV precision requires:
   - Choosing renormalization scheme
   - Choosing order of perturbative expansion
   - Choosing treatment of non-perturbative effects
   - Choosing hadronization model
   
   Each choice = axiomatic commitment

**Result:** The precision of m_t = 172.76 В± 0.30 GeV documents *how precisely the community has coordinated its axioms*, not how precisely nature "specifies" quark mass.

### 4.3 The Grammar of Scientific Consensus

Prime structures function as **consensus markers**:

**Simple prime products (2Г—3Г—5Г—7):**
- Indicate "first-approximation agreement"
- Multiple frameworks give similar results
- Low theoretical tension

**Complex prime structures (2вЃґГ—3ВІГ—7Г—137):**
- Indicate "negotiated precision"
- Specific corrections from specific frameworks
- High theoretical integration

**Changing prime structures (210в†’340):**
- Indicate "paradigm transition"
- Community adopting new axiomatic foundation
- Active renegotiation period

**Dual prime structures (Hв‚Ђ: 73 vs. 67):**
- Indicate "coexisting paradigms"
- Multiple frameworks not yet unified
- Structured disagreement

### 4.4 Constructivism Without Relativism

This view does not imply "anything goes" or "constants are arbitrary":

**What IS constructed:**
- The specific numerical value assigned
- The decimal precision claimed
- The interpretative framework used

**What is NOT constructed:**
- The empirical phenomena being described
- The need for numerical consistency
- The constraints imposed by experimental data

**Analogy:** Legal systems construct "property rights," but can't arbitrarily declare gravity doesn't affect buildings. Constants are like legal codes: constructed, yet constrained by external reality.
